Methi Thepla Recipe

A Delicious Gujarati Flatbread

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (methi), chopped
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 tsp ginger paste
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp oil (for dough)
  • Water (as needed to make dough)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps

  • Step 1: In a mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour, chopped methi leaves, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, red chili powder, carom seeds, ginger paste, and salt.
  • Step 2: Mix the ingredients together and add oil. Slowly add water, a little at a time, to form a smooth, soft dough.
  • Step 3: Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball).
  • Step 4: Roll each ball into a thin circle (about 6-8 inches in diameter) using a rolling pin, dusting with flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  • Step 5: Heat a tava or non-stick pan. Place the rolled-out dough onto the pan and cook for 1-2 minutes until small bubbles form on the surface.
  • Step 6: Flip the thepla and cook the other side. Apply oil or ghee on both sides and cook until golden brown spots appear.
  • Step 7: Remove from the pan and serve hot with yogurt, pickles, or chutney.
