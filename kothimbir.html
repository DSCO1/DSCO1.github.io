Kothimbir Vadi Recipe

A Popular Maharashtrian Snack

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups coriander leaves (kothimbir), chopped
  • 1/2 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda (optional for fluffiness)
  • 1 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • Water (to knead the dough)

Steps

  • Step 1: In a large mixing bowl, add chopped coriander leaves, chickpea flour, rice flour, chopped green chilies, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, ginger-garlic paste, and sesame seeds.
  • Step 2: Add salt and mix everything well. Gradually add water and knead to form a thick dough. If using, add baking soda to make it fluffier.
  • Step 3: Grease your hands and shape the dough into small logs (like sausages).
  • Step 4: Heat water in a steamer or a large pot, place the logs in a steaming basket, and steam for 20-25 minutes.
  • Step 5: Once the logs are cool enough to handle, cut them into thick slices.
  • Step 6: Heat oil in a pan, and fry the slices on both sides until golden brown and crispy.
  • Step 7: Serve hot with green chutney or tamarind chutney as a delicious snack.
